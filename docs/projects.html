NSF-REU Project: The Impact of Memory Non-Contiguity On Tensor Transformations In Pytorch

This project is the result of a ten week program that funds student research projects at Washington State University: Vancouver. My research focused on memory noncontiguity effects on PyTorch tensor performance and GPU efficiency. I designed and executed benchmarking experiments analyzing tensor layout and computational efficiency. At the end of the program the findings were presented at the WSU Summer Research Symposium to faculty and peer researchers.
